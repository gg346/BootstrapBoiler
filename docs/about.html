Origins of the email..

Between 1972 and the early 1980s, e-mail, or network mail as it was referred to, was discovered by thousands of early users. It all started when Len Kleinrock came back from the UK on a computing and communications conference. He forgot his razor and wanted to test out a method. There was a handy bit of software on the network called the resource-sharing executive. If you typed in “where so-and-so, the software looked for so-and-so by searching the “who” list—a roster of everyone logged on—at each site. You could locate a person on the network this way if he happened to be logged on at that moment. He was able to reach out and another US employee was able to bring his razor home the next day.

Little did they know..

TheARPANET’s creators didn’t have a grand vision for the invention of an earth-circling message-handling system. But once the first couple of dozen nodes were installed, early users turned the system of linked computers into a personal as well as a professional communications tool. Fast forward a few decades and now email is the number one source for communicating in the corporate and professional environment.

Gianluca Guarino 2018
